# Yelp Research Project

A comprehensive academic research project focused on analyzing the Yelp online review platform and its impact on consumer behavior, business decisions, and market dynamics. This repository contains systematic literature review materials, research datasets, and analytical frameworks.

## 🎯 Research Focus

This project investigates how online reviews and digital platforms influence:

- **Consumer Decision-Making**: How reviews affect consumer choices and satisfaction
- **Business Performance**: Impact of ratings and reviews on business survival, financing, and operations
- **Platform Design**: How platform features (rating systems, moderation, integration) affect user behavior
- **Market Dynamics**: Competition, spillover effects, and economic implications of review platforms
- **Social Impact**: Bias, discrimination, and social movements in online reviews

## 📊 Dataset

### Primary Data Sources

- **Yelp Dataset**: Comprehensive dataset containing:
  - Business reviews and ratings
  - User-generated photos
  - Business information and attributes
  - User demographics and behavior

- **Official Yelp Dataset**: Available at [https://business.yelp.com/data/resources/open-dataset/](https://business.yelp.com/data/resources/open-dataset/)

## 🎯 Key Findings

### Business Impact

- **Financial Consequences**: A 0.5-star increase in Yelp ratings leads to 25-basis-point decrease in loan spread and 6% lower collateral requirements
- **Survival Prediction**: Consumer-posted photos can predict restaurant survival up to 3 years (vs. 1 year for reviews)
- **Spillover Effects**: Platform activities create significant economic spillovers for complementary local businesses

### Consumer Behavior

- **Negativity Bias**: Positive reviews are less valued than negative reviews due to attribution differences
- **Photo Impact**: Photos increase review helpfulness, especially when photo-text similarity is high
- **Expert vs. Novice**: Reviewing experts show restraint from extreme ratings, affecting aggregate metrics

### Platform Dynamics

- **Review Fraud**: ~16% of restaurant reviews are filtered; fraud more likely when reputation is weak
- **Social Integration**: Facebook integration increases review volume but may reduce quality
- **Multidimensional Ratings**: Enhance informativeness and consumer satisfaction

### Social Impact

- **Bias Effects**: Status awards reduce gender bias in ratings by 56.5%
- **Racial Discrimination**: COVID-19 led to increased discrimination against Chinese restaurants, followed by anti-discrimination responses
- **Economic Disparities**: Sharing economy benefits disproportionately affect areas with more White residents
