Research

02 / 06

My research lies at the intersection of marketing strategy and consumer behavior, with a focus on digital marketing, brand activism, and artificial intelligence in marketing. I employ quantitative methods—experimental design, causal inference, and machine learning—to investigate strategic phenomena in digital environments.

Research Streams

Brand Activism

Investigating brand activism in non-Western institutional contexts and its impact on consumer and investor behavior. My work introduces the concept of System-Threat Activism (STA) and examines its effects on consumer responses through identity-threat mechanisms.

AI, Digital Platforms & Marketing Analytics

Examining artificial intelligence and algorithmic systems in digital marketing environments using large-scale data and deep learning. This stream explores influencer marketing effectiveness and algorithmic valuation anomalies in digital platforms.

Teaching

03 / 06

I have accumulated 300 working hours as a Teaching Assistant across undergraduate and postgraduate courses at the University of Liverpool. Approximately 80% of my TA work involves seminar and online support for statistics, text analysis, and sentiment analysis using RStudio. I also handle marking of assignments, proposals, infographics, and presentations.

Teaching Philosophy

I focus on hands-on support, helping students develop analytical skills through practical engagement with real-world marketing problems and industry datasets. My approach emphasises active learning and problem-solving, enabling students to generate actionable managerial insights from data.
