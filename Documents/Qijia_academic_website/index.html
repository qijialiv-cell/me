Research

My research lies at the intersection of marketing strategy and consumer behavior, with a focus on digital marketing, brand activism, and artificial intelligence in marketing. I employ quantitative methods including experimental design, causal inference, and machine learning to investigate strategic marketing phenomena in digital environments.

Research Streams

Brand Activism

Investigating brand activism in non-Western institutional contexts and its impact on consumer and investor behavior. My work introduces the concept of System-Threat Activism (STA) and examines its effects on consumer responses through identity threat mechanisms.

AI, Digital Platforms & Marketing Analytics

Examining artificial intelligence and algorithmic systems in digital marketing environments using large-scale data and deep learning. This research stream explores influencer marketing effectiveness and algorithmic valuation anomalies in digital platforms.

Teaching

A dedicated educator with over 300 hours of teaching experience, I have taught courses across multiple academic levels. My teaching philosophy emphasizes hands-on engagement with real-world marketing problems and industry datasets, enabling students to develop analytical skills and generate actionable managerial insights.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is informed by Biggs' Constructive Alignment framework, ensuring that learning outcomes, teaching activities, and assessments are aligned with industry-relevant marketing competencies particularly in digital marketing, marketing strategy, and marketing analytics.
